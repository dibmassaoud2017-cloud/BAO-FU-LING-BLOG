## The Big Question: Is Camphor Cream Safe for Eczema?

Yes, but with important rules. Its safety hinges on three main factors: **concentration**, **application site**, and **product quality**.

### Key Safety Guidelines
*   **Check the Concentration**: The FDA allows topical analgesics to contain between 3% and 11% camphor. Anything higher can be too harsh. Never use pure camphor oil directly on the skin.
*   **Never Apply to Broken Skin**: This is the most crucial rule. Applying camphor cream to open sores, weeping eczema, or cracked skin can cause stinging and allow the camphor to be absorbed into the bloodstream, which can be toxic. Only apply it to intact, though irritated, skin.
*   **External Use Only**: Camphor is toxic if ingested. Keep it away from your mouth, eyes, and nostrils. Always wash your hands after application (unless your hands are the treatment area).
*   **Perform a Patch Test**: Before using any new product, apply a small amount to a discreet area of skin (like your inner wrist) and wait 24 hours to check for any adverse reaction.

When you follow these guidelines and choose a well-formulated product, camphor cream can be an excellent tool in your eczema management kit.
